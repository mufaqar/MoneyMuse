Post anatomy
1
Hook (line 1 — the most important)
Stop the scroll. Ask a question, make a bold claim, or reveal a surprising number. Must work without an image.
"Most people will work 40 years and still retire broke. Here's the one thing they skipped…"
2
Body — value delivery (3–7 points)
Use short lines, emojis as bullets, and white space. Each point = one idea. No walls of text.
"✅ Needs: 50% (rent, food, bills) ✅ Wants: 30% (fun, dining out) ✅ Savings: 20% (invest + emergency fund)"
3
Bridge — make it personal
One sentence that connects the info to the reader's life. Builds trust and relatability.
"This simple split changed how I look at every paycheck."
4
Call to action (CTA)
Always end with an action. Comment, save, share, or tag someone. One CTA only — never stack multiple.
"Which bucket do you struggle with most? Drop it in the comments 👇"
5
Hashtags (3–5 max on Facebook)
Keep them relevant and not spammy. Facebook penalizes heavy hashtag use unlike Instagram.
#MoneyMuse #PersonalFinance #BudgetingTips

Best practices for Facebook
Best posting times
Wed–Fri, 9–11 AM or 6–8 PM local time. Avoid weekends for finance content.
Image format
1200×630px for feed posts. Use branded green + dark navy from your cover design.
Post length
150–300 words sweet spot. Facebook shows "See more" after ~3 lines — hook must land before that.
Posting frequency
3–5 posts per week. Mix: 2 educational posts, 1 reel, 1 engagement post, 1 motivational.
Reuse this exact structure for any topic — just swap the hook, swap the bullet points, keep the CTA at the end.
